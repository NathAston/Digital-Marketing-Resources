LinkedIn, possibly my favourite Social Network and an absolute goldmine for growing a business and reaching leads. Today, I'm going to teach you how I grew my account to 58,000+ connections on LinkedIn and how you can use LinkedIn to gain leads and website traffic on Autopilot.

If you are reading this, I'm assuming you already have a LinkedIn account, so head to the dashboar and use the search bar to look for your target audience. We are going to be using boolean search which is going to look something like this. ("Digital Marketer" OR "Growth Hacker") AND ("conversion" OR "SEO" OR "social media") this is a fairly broad search, which is going to return all of the users in the LinkedIn Database that are either digital marketers, growth hackers and have the keywords conversion, seo of social media somewhere in there profile.

This is literally my exact target audience, and LinkedIn is displaying them right to me! Next, we are probably going to want to narrow down the countries, if you only trade locally of course you just want to target your own city. We are then going to download a little Google Chrome extension called LinkedHelper, then set up an Auto Follow campaign and display a personal message something like this.. Hey ==first-name==, I'm looking to Network with other Growth Hackerts in the XYZ area! Would be great to connect :) Nathan https://Notifia.io

I always just put my site in the signature, we aren't pushing them towards it or asking them to click on it (but most people do out of curiosity and to see a bit of what I'm about) this is already driving traffic to our site. Considering I used to send out about 600 of these messages per day, I was getting around 300 unique visitors to my site matching my target audience everyday from one LinkedIn account. If you scale this out and start operate a LinkedIn messaging farm across say 5-10 accounts, that's a huge amount of targeted traffic you are sending to your site everyday from a simple invite message.

TIP: I don't reccomend sending 600 messages per day, especially when you first start out.

If your account is new, really you want to start by sending around 20 connection requests per day for a few days, then up to maybe 50, then 100 and I would leave it at 100. It's better to see consistent growth and LinkedIn is fare more strict than they used to be.

Keep sending out connection requests and growing your network. Tip: you can set up saved searches, so LinkedIn will notify you whenever someone new matches your search so you can connect with them instantly.
