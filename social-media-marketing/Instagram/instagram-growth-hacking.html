Growing Instagram Followers

Follow / Unfollow Method

Probably the most common method I see for people growing followers on Instagram is the follow unfollow method. I will be completely open and honest, short term - yes, this method does work you will see consistent follower growth (I grew my personal Instagram to 45,000+ followers using this) however, long term you are doing more harm than good. You will see your engagement drop off, Instagram will not promote your posts and in all honesty it's not worth doing. Instagram is investing heavily in fighting these actions, the algorithms are extremely efficent in picking up trends of someone using the follow unfollow method. Most likely, you will not be banned (most people believe they haven't been caught as their account hasn't been banned). But you will see your engagement drop tremendously as instagram slowly phases out your accounts reach, which defeats the whole purpose of growing followers in the first place.

If you are going to use this method, I would create multiple "slave accounts" to perform the follow/unfollow actions and send automated direct messages inviting people to follow your main account. This way the Instagram algorithms won't pick up on your main account as it's not performing any of the marketing activity itself, and to be caught doing this

Is your brand suitable for Instagram

This is a question you should be asking yourself from the first stages, by all means you should create a profile and have some presence on Instagram. But before you invest a lot of time and energy into marketing on Instagram, you may want to ask yourselves these questions - if your brand doesn't quite match up, you may find your time is better invested on other Social Platforms such as LinkedIn

This really comes down to your niche/brand, if you are in fashion and are reguarly doing photoshoots or another creative Industry Instagram is going to be the perfect platform for you to market your brand.

If you aren't able to do this, you could look at more creative visuals such as motiviational images, quotes or copyright free photography (Unsplash is a great source for this, you could easily grow a very high-quality Instagram from their content alone)

If you aren't sure what to post, look for influencers within your niche and research your competitiors to see what type of content they are posting, look at the likes/comments on the posts to see which performs best and use this as inspiration.


Engaging with users

Whilst I have expressed my feelings towards the follow/unfollow method, the principle behind the method is very effective. Which is simply engaging with users in your target market in a hope they will follow you back, this is very effective and is the best way to grow your audience, particuarly at early stages.

Find popular hashtags withing your niche, for exmaple if I was looking to grow Notifia's users via Instagram I would use #growthhacking, and go an like and comment on any users posts matching this. You can set up Automation tools to do this if you wish.

Using Hashtags Effectively

Use Hashtags with 500,000-1M Posts that are related to your niche, these are popular enough to get noticed, but not too large that you'll get drowned out within a second by the competition.

Improving Engagement

At this present time, the Instagram algorithms major ranking factor is engagement. Every time you post, your goal is to get as many users to like, comment and click on your post as possible, this delivers postivie signals to the algorithm and maximises your chances of ranking your post.

Instruct your users - I'm sure you've seen posts go viral that say "tag 3 friends" or "double tap if you agree" that's because these posts are prompting users to engage, and they are surprisingly effective.

Creating Content

be sure to make use of instagrams latest features whenever they release one, carousel images, Instagram Stories and live video all perform extremely well and are naturally promoted by instagram. If you are trying ot grow a local audience, Stories work especially well for this.
